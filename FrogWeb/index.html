Frog Fun Facts

  • Frogs are amphibians, which means they can live both in water and on land.
  • There are over 6,000 species of frogs worldwide, found on every continent except Antarctica.
  • The smallest frog is the Paedophryne amauensis, which measures only about 7.7 mm in length.
  • The Goliath frog, native to West Africa, is the largest frog species and can grow up to 32 cm in length.
  • Frogs don't typically drink water; they absorb it through their skin.
  • Frogs are known for their jumping abilities; some species can leap over 20 times their body length.
  • Many frogs have vibrant colors as a warning to predators that they are toxic or distasteful.
  • Some frog species can change their skin color to blend in with their surroundings, aiding in camouflage.
  • Female frogs are often larger than males in many species.
  • Frogs have been symbols of transformation and fertility in many cultures throughout history.
